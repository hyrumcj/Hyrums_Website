About This Hobby

Wheat Farming in the American West

Wheat farming is one of the most demanding and rewarding agricultural pursuits in the United States. From the rolling plains of Kansas to the high desert valleys of Utah, cultivating wheat demands knowledge of soil science, weather patterns, equipment mechanics, and sheer physical endurance. This is the work that shaped me.

The Full Process

What It Takes to Run a Wheat Farm

  1. Clear the Ground

    • Remove rocks, debris, and previous crop residue
    • Deep plow to break up hardpan layers
    • Test soil pH and nutrient levels before any prep work

  2. Cultivate

    • Apply fertilizer and soil amendments based on test results
    • Disk and harrow to create a fine, even seedbed
    • Control weeds with herbicide or mechanical tillage

  3. Plant the Wheat

    • Select the right winter or spring variety for your climate
    • Calibrate the grain drill for proper seed depth (1–1.5 inches)
    • Seed at the optimal rate for your soil type and region

  4. Harvest the Wheat

    • Monitor grain moisture — target 13–14% for harvest
    • Set combine header height and reel speed for minimum loss
    • Watch weather windows carefully; rain on cut wheat is costly

  5. Store the Wheat

    • Clean grain bins thoroughly before filling
    • Maintain grain moisture below 13% to prevent spoilage
    • Monitor temperature and run aeration fans during hot months
